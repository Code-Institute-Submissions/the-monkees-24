Davy Jones

In 1966, Davy Jones was given a role in the sitcom, 'The Monkees'. With this sticom, he made 58 television episodes, 9 albums, a television special, and a movie before calling it quits in the 1970s. Because of his popularity with The Monkees, another singer named David Jones was forced to change his name to David Bowie. He rejoined The Monkees from 1986-89 and 1996-97. Davy Jones died at 66 from a heart attack on February 29, 2012.

Peter Tork

Peter Tork performed as a folk musician in Greenwich Village and Los Angeles before being selected for The Monkees TV show. He made 58 TV episodes, 6 albums, a TV special, and a movie before leaving The Monkees in 1969. He also released a solo single in 1982. In 1986 to 1989, he rejoined The Monkees for tours and in 1994, he released his first solo album, "Stranger Things Have Happened". Peter Tork was diagnosed with a rare form of head and neck cancer and underwent surgery to remove a growth on his tongue in 2009. He made a full recovery.

Michael Nesmith

Michael Nesmith fashioned a diverse career within music and also film. Nesmith was self-described as a "failure" growing up. "I just didn't do anything", he said in his famous 1965 screen test for The Monkees in 1966. When Nesmith won the role for The Monkees, he was the first involved to saee where the show and the music would go. Nesmith also invented the idea for what would become MTV. He sold the idea to Time Warner and created a proof of concept for a 24 hour music television channel in the form of 6 half hour shows called "Pop Clips".>

Micky Dolenz

Micky first established himself as a performer at the age of 10 under the stage name of 'Micky Braddock', he starred in his first TV series, Circus Boy, which aired from 1956 to 1958. He also learned how to play guitar and performed with a number of rock 'n' roll bands, including one called The Missing Links. The Monkees' debut single, "Last Train To Clarksville" featuring Micky on lead vocals, hit the charts on September 10, 1966 and rocketed to number one, two days later.
